MAHE's Online MBA: What 70+ Years of Legacy Actually Buys You

MAHE gets described in marketing copy as having "70+ years of legacy" constantly. Here's what that actually translates to for someone evaluating its Online MBA against newer entrants.

Manipal Academy of Higher Education was established in 1953 and has grown into a multi-campus institution — Manipal, Mangaluru, Bengaluru, Jamshedpur, and a Dubai campus — with over 35,000 students from 60+ countries and Institution of Eminence status granted in 2020.

Scale, not just age

The "legacy" framing undersells the more relevant point: MAHE runs roughly 300+ programs across 31+ streams today, which means its online MBA sits inside an institution with genuine breadth in health sciences, engineering, and management — not a single-purpose online degree shop.

What this means for placement networks

A larger, older, multi-campus institution typically carries a wider alumni and corporate-relationship base than a newer online-only provider, which matters more for the placement-assistance side of an MBA than for the coursework itself.

The practical trade-off

MAHE's Online MBA sits in the same general price and format range as its DLP peers (Manipal University Jaipur, Sikkim Manipal University) — the three campuses under the Manipal Education Group umbrella share overlapping specialisations, so the real decision factor is often campus-specific faculty and elective availability rather than the MBA credential itself.
