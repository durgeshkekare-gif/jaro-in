Jaro Education's 17-Year Story: From Startup to Listed Edtech

Most edtech companies were built during the funding boom of the 2010s. Jaro Education wasn't — and that timeline explains a lot about how the company operates today.

Jaro Education was founded in 2009, years before "edtech" was a term investors chased. It grew as a self-funded, profitable business from the outset, which is a different starting position than most Indian edtech companies that scaled on venture money and only later worried about unit economics.

Profitable from day one

That early discipline shows up in the numbers the company has since made public. Jaro has stayed profitable across its operating history, and more recently posted a 342% jump in profit after tax over three years — a growth curve driven more by expanding institutional partnerships than by discounting.

Going public

In 2025, Jaro Institute of Technology Management & Research Ltd — the entity behind Jaro Education — listed on the NSE and BSE. The IPO was oversubscribed roughly 23 times, reflecting investor appetite for a company with a 15+ year profitable track record rather than a growth-at-any-cost story.

Where the company stands now

Today, Jaro works with more than 25 partner institutes spanning IIMs, IITs, and DLP universities, and counts over 3.5 lakh learners as alumni. Roughly half the workforce is women, with several in leadership roles — a detail the company has leaned into in recent employer-branding campaigns.

The throughline across 17 years is consistent: partner with established institutions rather than build a parallel credentialing system, and let the partnerships do the credibility work that a young platform brand couldn't do alone.
